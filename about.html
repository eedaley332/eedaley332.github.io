About Captain and Crew


Don

I am a recently retired business owner and longtime upstate New York resident, who grew up spending summers on Lake George. Once retired, I wanted to do something part-time that I enjoyed. The lake was calling!

I felt my knowledge of the lake as well as owning a comfortable 25 foot Chaparral open bow power boat, that seats 10, would allow couples or small families a unique opportunity to tour the lake and it's wonderful islands up close and personal.

Lucy

A second passion of mine is also cooking! Please ask about the free gourmet picnic lunch available with a wide variety of choices. We are here to please you and make your visit on the lake a special one.

About Lake George


Lake George is a town in upstate New Yorkâ€™s Adirondack mountain range. The lake itself is 32.2 miles long with over 170 islands. It is nicknamed the Queen of American Lakes. At the southern end of the lake lies Fort William Henry, which was constructed in 1755 after the Battle of Lake George in the French and Indian War. You can follow the long, narrow lake up to Ticonderoga, located on the northern end.
